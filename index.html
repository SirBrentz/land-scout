How to read the verdict

  • Cheapest 10% is not automatically a bargain — land that cheap usually has a reason: no legal access, floodway, timber just cut, tax-deed title, or terrain you can't use. Run the §5 checklist twice as hard.
  • Below the 25th percentile with no red flags is genuine deal territory — these get bid fast; this is what the daily email alert exists for.
  • Typical (25th–75th) means the ask is normal. Your leverage is negotiation: sold prices run 40–60% of asking medians in this market, so offering 10–20% under a "typical" ask is standard, not insulting.
  • Above the 75th percentile — the seller is pricing in something (frontage, utilities, views, a pond) or just overreaching. Make them show you which.

5Due-diligence checklist (Arkansas-specific)

  1. Legal access. Deeded access or a recorded easement — not neighborly permission. Landlocked parcels are a top reason land looks cheap.
  2. Soil / septic. Arkansas has no minimum lot size for septic — buildability is decided by a formal ADH soil-pit evaluation (depth to rock, impervious layers, groundwater), not a simple perc test. Poor soils can force an alternative system with a 3-acre minimum. Make offers contingent on a soil evaluation.
  3. Flood zones. Check FEMA maps (msc.fema.gov). Creek frontage is a feature until it's a floodway.
  4. Survey. Rural tracts sell off old descriptions — require a current boundary survey.
  5. Mineral & timber rights. "Mineral rights reserved" means they don't convey. Freshly cut timber means the seller already took that value.
  6. Covenants / POA. Unzoned ≠ unrestricted; get the title search early (AREC recommends an attorney for any land contract).
  7. Utilities. Electric-line extension costs real money per pole; much of the ring is well country. "Utilities at road" is worth paying for.
  8. Tax classification. Ag/pasture/timber land is assessed at use value (soil productivity), not market value — keep the classification and holding costs stay low.

6Where land actually trades

  • Aggregators: LandWatch, LandSearch, Land.com. The tracker watches LandWatch + LandSearch daily.
  • Specialist brokerages: Mossy Oak Properties (294 active AR listings at research time), United Country, Whitetail Properties, local Conway agents — the pocket-listing channel once you know your target.
  • COSL tax-delinquent auctions (cosl.org): minimum bid = back taxes + costs. Sept 2025 regional auction: 137 of 504 parcels for $287,318 (~$2,100/parcel). But — limited warranty deed, no title insurance, 90-day litigation window, owner can redeem until the day before, and a quiet-title suit is usually needed for marketable title. Cheap raw hunting dirt with an attorney; wrong channel for a homesite.

7Financing reality

Credit-union land loans are strict: representative Arkansas terms (AFCU) are 25% down minimum, higher-than-mortgage rates, balloon structures — and capped at 10 acres. For 15–80+ acre tracts the real paths are Farm Credit lenders (AgHeritage FCS, Farm Credit of Western Arkansas — no acreage caps), owner financing (common here; attorney-review any contract-for-deed), or cash. Listings advertising owner financing get a scoring bonus in the tracker.

8Hunting factors

Camp Robinson WMA — 9,319 acres in Faulkner/Pulaski, inside the ring (AGFC permit + gate check-in; active military installation). The Cadron Creek corridors and Ozark NF fringe anchor habitat to the north — exactly where the cheap 40–80+ acre tracts are. Note the top deal on the board borders Gulf Mountain WMA (Van Buren) — adjacency to public ground effectively extends your huntable acreage for free. Treat 20 ac as a practical floor, 40+ as comfortable, and value adjacency to big timber over raw acreage.
