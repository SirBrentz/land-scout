**What this tells you:**
- **Small parcels carry a huge per-acre premium.** 5–15 ac tracts run 2–4× the per-acre price of 40+ ac tracts in the same county, because they're sold as homesites. If your goal is hunting/recreation acreage, buying bigger is dramatically more efficient per acre.
- **Van Buren County is the value play** for hunting/camping/investment acreage: 80 ac there costs about what 15 ac costs in Faulkner.
- **Faulkner commands a commute premium** (Conway metro growth). If the future homesite matters most, Faulkner/southern Cleburne makes sense; if acreage matters most, go north/west.
- Independent cross-check: the deep-research pass verified individual Faulkner 15–40 ac listings at $6,000–$8,600/acre ([18.9 ac Enola @ $8,619/ac](https://www.landwatch.com/arkansas-land-for-sale/enola); [15.5 ac Quitman @ $6,387/ac after a $10k drop](https://www.remax.com/ar/quitman/home-details/12-countryside-dr-quitman-ar-72131)). Those sit at the cheap end of our $15,000 Faulkner median — both are real; listing mix explains the spread. Expect **good** Faulkner 15–40 ac deals in the $6–9k/ac range, and the median asking closer to $15k.
- ⚠️ Published county averages (e.g. "Faulkner averages $35,580/acre" on LandSearch) blend in subdivision lots and homes — never use them to judge a land deal. (Verified: rural-only averages are ~$16–17k/ac vs $63k/ac for houses-with-land on the same site.)

## 3. Due-diligence checklist (Arkansas-specific, all verified)

Before any offer, in rough order of deal-killing power:

1. **Legal access.** Confirm deeded access or recorded easement — not "the neighbor's always allowed it." Landlocked parcels exist and are a big reason land looks cheap. Red-flag phrases: *no legal access, easement needed, access by permission*.
2. **Soil / septic (if you might ever build).** Arkansas has **no minimum lot size for septic** — buildability is decided by a formal **soil pit evaluation** (depth to rock, impervious layers, seasonal groundwater), done by an ADH Designated Representative + Environmental Specialist. A simple "perc test" is only a rough estimate. Poor soils can force an alternative surface-discharge system that carries a **3-acre minimum** for new construction. Budget a soil evaluation *before* closing; make the offer contingent on it. ([ADH Onsite Wastewater FAQ](https://healthy.arkansas.gov/programs-services/public-health-safety/onsite-wastewater/onsite-wastewater-faqs/))
3. **Flood zones.** Check the FEMA map for the parcel (msc.fema.gov). Creek frontage is a feature until it's a floodway.
4. **Survey.** Rural tracts routinely sell off old descriptions. Get a current boundary survey or make the sale contingent on one.
5. **Mineral/timber rights.** Ask explicitly whether minerals convey; *mineral rights reserved* in a listing means they don't. Recently cut timber = the seller already took the timber value.
6. **Covenants/POA.** Even unzoned rural land can carry recorded restrictions or a property owners' association. Get the title search early (the Arkansas Real Estate Commission recommends a title search + attorney for any land contract).
7. **Utilities.** Distance to electric matters ($/pole for extension adds up fast); water = well country in much of the ring. "Utilities at road" is worth real money.
8. **Property tax classification.** Arkansas assesses ag/pasture/timber land at **use value** (soil productivity, 8–12% cap rate), not market value — keep the classification and holding costs stay low ([Ark. Code § 26-26-407](https://law.justia.com/codes/arkansas/title-26/subtitle-3/chapter-26/subchapter-4/section-26-26-407/), [Timber Tax summary](https://www.timbertax.org/statetaxes/states/proptax/arkansas/)).

## 4. Where land actually trades here

- **Aggregators:** LandWatch, LandSearch, Land.com (LandWatch & Land.com are both CoStar). Zillow/Realtor carry some rural land but land-specialty sites have the depth. **Your tracker now watches LandWatch + LandSearch daily.**
- **Specialist brokerages:** Mossy Oak Properties (294 active AR listings at research time), United Country, Whitetail Properties, plus local Conway-area agents. These get pocket/early listings — worth one relationship once you know your target.
- **COSL tax-delinquent auctions** (cosl.org) — the wildcard channel:
  - Minimum bid = back taxes + penalties + costs (not market value). Cheap entry is real: the Sept 2025 Faulkner/Cleburne/Van Buren auction sold 137 of 504 parcels for $287,318 total (~$2,100/parcel average).
  - Faulkner parcels sold hot (18 of 23, ~$9,164 avg); Van Buren supplied bulk cheap inventory (72 of 380 sold, ~$1,024 avg).
  - **Serious caveats, all verified:** you get only a **limited warranty deed**, no title insurance available at purchase, a **90-day litigation window** (make no improvements), owners can redeem the parcel until 4 p.m. the last business day *before* the auction, and you'll typically need a **quiet-title suit** to get marketable title (otherwise it takes 15+ years of tax payments under Ark. Code § 18-12-609). Unsold parcels hit the online **post-auction list** 30 days later — a lower-competition channel. ([COSL Buyers page](https://www.cosl.org/Home/Buyers))
  - Verdict: fine for cheap raw hunting dirt if you go in eyes-open with an attorney; wrong channel for a future homesite.

## 5. Buying mechanics / financing (verified)

- **Bank/credit-union land loans are strict:** representative Arkansas terms (AFCU) are **25% minimum down**, higher rates than mortgages, **balloon structures** (10-yr am/3-yr balloon, 15/4, 20/5), and — the kicker — **capped at 10 acres** ([AFCU land loans](https://www.afcu.org/mortgage/mortgages/land-loans/)).
- **For 15–80+ acre tracts** you'll be looking at **Farm Credit lenders** (AgHeritage FCS, Farm Credit of Western Arkansas — fixed rates, no acreage caps, land+construction combos), **owner financing** (common in rural listings; have an Arkansas attorney review any contract-for-deed per AREC), or **cash**.
- Practical read: if you buy at ~$100k, plan on ~$25k down + closing (title insurance, survey, soil eval) if financing, or negotiate owner terms — listings advertising *owner financing* deserve a scoring bonus, which the tracker now applies.

## 6. Hunting-specific factors

- **Camp Robinson WMA** — 9,319 acres (National Guard land, AGFC Zone 110) straddling Faulkner/Pulaski, right inside your ring. Access requires an AGFC General Use Permit + gate check-in (active military installation), but private land near it benefits from the deer herd ([AGFC page](https://www.agfc.com/wma/camp-robinson-wma/)).
- Cadron Creek corridors and the Ozark NF fringe (north edge of the ring, Van Buren/Cleburne) anchor the wildlife habitat story — and that's exactly where the cheap 40–80+ ac tracts are.
- No claim about a "minimum huntable acreage" survived verification — treat 20 ac as a practical floor for bow/limited rifle use and 40+ as comfortable; adjacency to big timber blocks matters more than raw acreage.
- Holding-cost bonus: keep bought land in timber/ag classification and taxes stay tied to productivity, not your purchase price (§3.8 above).

## Confidence notes & gaps

- Benchmarks are **asking** prices, one-day snapshot; sold prices run lower (Land.com's Faulkner sold-record median was ~$6,132/ac vs $15,901/ac asking — verified). Expect to negotiate.
- Three research claims were **refuted and excluded**: a supposed 344-ac Greenbrier comp, a "$1,000–2,500/ac Ozark baseline," and specific water-frontage premium examples. Water/timber/utility premiums are directionally real but unquantified here.
- Perry County 40–80 ac has only 2 listings behind it — thin sample.
- Farm Credit actual terms (down payment/rate for 15–80 ac recreational tracts) not yet verified — worth a phone call when you're ready; that's the financing path most likely to fit.
- Small LandSearch caveat: its cards don't flag houses, so a few Faulkner "raw land" medians may include improved parcels; LandWatch entries (which do flag houses) are filtered clean.
